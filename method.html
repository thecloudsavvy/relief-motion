The Relief Motion Method

A structured, evidence-based operating model for post‑acute rehabilitation—designed for predictable outcomes, clinical integration, and scalable delivery.

Framework

The 4‑Phase Neuro‑Recovery Protocol

Our neuro pathway standardises post‑stroke and neurological rehabilitation through phase‑based progression, measurable milestones, and structured documentation.

Phase 1 Stabilise & Baseline

Assessment + Risk Control

Clinical screening, baseline functional measures, goal setting, and safety planning for early recovery.

  • Red‑flag screening and escalation criteria
  • Baseline mobility, balance, and ADL measures
  • Caregiver education and home safety plan
Phase 2 Restore Capacity

Re‑Conditioning + Control

Targeted mobility restoration, postural control, strength re‑conditioning, and motor learning principles.

  • Task‑specific training and gait retraining
  • Strength, endurance, and balance progression
  • Home exercise adherence support
Phase 3 Return‑to‑Function

Functional Independence

Transfer training, community ambulation preparation, and ADL optimisation toward independence.

  • Stair training and real‑world movement patterns
  • Dual‑task balance and fall‑risk reduction
  • Family training and progression safeguards
Phase 4 Maintain & Monitor

Hybrid Follow‑Up

Tele‑rehab check‑ins, maintenance programming, and monitoring to sustain gains and reduce regression.

  • Remote follow‑ups and adherence reviews
  • Maintenance plan aligned to goals
  • Discharge summary with next‑step guidance

What “Program‑First” Means

Orthopedic, post‑operative, and geriatric pathways are delivered using the same operating principles: measurable baselines, phased progression, and clinician‑grade documentation.

Documentation

Measurable Milestones

Functional targets aligned to the condition and care plan—not generic session goals.

Hybrid Follow‑Up

Tele‑rehab check‑ins that keep patients on track between in‑person sessions.
